Q5.Describe the process of evolution of the Earth.
Ans: The earth has reached its present form through several phases.
a. The earth began as a ball of swirling dust and clouds and passed through a molten stage.
b .The size of the earth grew, when the hot earth was constantly bombarded by massive rocks and
other materials from the space.
c. When the earth was in the molten form, the heavier particles went to the bottom of the earth and
formed the core.
d. The lighter particles cooled at the top and formed the top layer or crust.
e. As the earth's interior continued to cool, it contracted and the outer crust wrinkled forming ridges-
the mountains and basins-the oceans.
f. Different kinds of gases including water vapour formed the atmosphere on earth.
Q6. Describe the internal structure of the earth. (Draw fig. 1.3 T.B)
Ans: The earth is made up of three main layers.
A) Crust:
i. The outer part of the earth on which we live is called the crust.
ii. While the earth was boiling in a molten form the lighter particles cooled at the top and formed the
layer of crust.
iii. The crust mostly consists of various kinds of rocks.
iv .This layer goes up to a depth of 100 kms.
B) Mantle:
i. While the earth was boiling in a molten form, the particles which were less heavy, settled in the
middle and formed the mantle.
ii. The upper part of the mantle is a pliable layer over which the crust floats.
Iii .This layer consists mainly of chemicals called silicates.
iv. It exists at the depths from 100 kms to 2900 kms.
C) Core:
i. While the earth was boiling in a molten form the heaviest substances formed the core.
ii. It is composed of dense and heavy substances like lron and Nickel.
iii. It exists at the depths from 2,900 kms to 6,376 kms.
iv .It can be divided into two sub-layers.
a) Outer core: This layer is composed of liquid metallic material like nickel and iron. It is 2,900-5,100
kms in thickness.
b) Inner core: The solid inner core is made up of iron compounds and heavy substances like gold. It is
5,100-6,376 kms in thickness.

c) Pangea, the hypothetical continent, from which present continents originated by the drift from
Mesozoic era to the present.
d) Wagener hypothesized the supercontinent of Pangea broke up to form:
a) Laurasia- consisting of present North America, Greenland and all of Eurasia, north of Indian

b) Gondwana land- consisting of present South America, Africa, Madagascar, Arabia, India, Malaysia,

Q10) Why does every country have its own standard time?
Ans: a.) The world is divided into 24 time zones.
b) The width of each time zone is 15 degrees of longitude.
c) This means that the difference between one time zone and the next is 1 hour.
d) Time zones of any country depend on the number of longitudes that pass through that country.
e) Some big countries have more than one time zone passing through them.
f) So it is necessary for a standard time which will be the same for the entire country to avoid
confusion in time from east to west
g) That is why some countries choose the time along one of the meridian that passes throughout the
territory and follow the time of that meridian for the whole country.
h) This time is called standard time.

Q12) When it is 12 noon in Greenwich (0), what is the local time at Mumbai (73E)?
Ans: Mumbai (73E): Local time at Mumbai is: 73 X 4=292 minutes, i.e. 4 hours 52 minutes. That
means the local time at Mumbai is 4 hours 52 minutes after 12 noon
i.e., 12.00 + 4.52=4.52 p.m. (EGA)

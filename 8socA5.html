Ls 5 : Forests : Using & Protecting Them
I.Keywords :
1.Afforestation : The process of planting trees in order to cover a forest area.
2.Deforestation : Clearing an area of forest by cutting down trees.
3.Forest Management : Forest Management is a branch of foresty , concerned with the overall
administrative , economic and legal aspect specially protection and forest regulation.
4.Forest Rights Act : The Government of India inacted Forest Rights Act in 2006. This act was
introduced to protect the rights of the tribals over the forests.
5.Reserve Forest : Reserve forests in India are considered as rich source of flora and fauna which
help in maintaining biological balance in the environment.
6.Forest Department : It is a department of government which is concerned with the protection,
development and implementation of the acts of forests in the country.

III. Answer the following questions.
4. Why do trees in Deciduous forests shed their leaves ?
Ans : The trees in Deciduous forests shed their leaves during dry months. As the water evaporates
through the leaves,they try to conserve moisture by getting rid of the leaves during the dry months.
New leaves come with the onset of rains so that the trees can produce food for themselves.

IV.Answer the following questions.
6. Classify the different types of forests and explain them.
Ans : Forests are natural resources. They are mainly divided into four types. They are :-
a) Evergreen Forests : These are dense forests with a variety of trees, plants, creepers etc. These
regions get very high rainfall and also have a very warm climate hence, have evergreen forests. They
are always green as the trees shed their leaves but get new leaves very soon. Jamun, Canes,
Bamboos, Kadam etc are some of the trees of these forests. Evergreen forest in the Himalayan
region is called Coniferous forests.They have leaves which are thin and shaped like needles .
b) Deciduous Forests : These forests grow in regions where it rains in only for some months and is
warm and dry during most of the year. The trees shed their leaves during very dry months.Water
evaporates through the leaves and the trees try to conserve moisture by getting rid of the leaves
during the dry months. Most of the forests in Telangana fall in this category.In areas with less rainfall
we have trees like Maddi, Teak, Velaga, Aegisa, Yepi Tuniki Neem etc.
c) Thorny Forests : These forests grow in very dry areas with little rainfall and high temperature.
Most of the trees are thorny. The common trees are Babul, Bulusu, Regu, Seetaphal etc.Thorny trees
have very tiny leaves and thorns which help to conserve water.
d) Littoral (sea coast) and Swamp Forests : These forests mostly grow in the sea coasts or on sandy
beach and marshy lands and on lands affected by tidal waves.Here the trees are adapted to salt
waters and the flow of tidal waves.These are also called Mangrove forests- the trees have developed
som unique features to survive in this difficult environment. Uppu ponna, Boddu ponna, Gundu
mada are some examples.

Ls. No 4 : THE POLAR REGIONS
I. Keywords:
1. Arctic Circle: 66 ½ degree North latitude on the globe is called the Arctic Circle.
2. Tundra vegetation: Tundra means very cold. The Tundra region gets very little sunlight; it has a
unique type of vegetation known as Tundra vegetation.
3. Icebergs: Icebergs are large chunks of ice that break off from glaciers and float into the ocean.
4. Eskimos: They are the indigenous people who live in scattered settlements in Greenland, Canada,
Alaska and Siberia.
5. Kayaks: Kayaks are small canoe like boats made of animals skins stretched over a wooden frame.
6. Igloo: It is an Eskimo word meaning shelter. It is made of snow.
7. Permafrost: Due to the cold, the upper surface of the soil in the Polar region is frozen into a hard
rock. This is called ‘Permafrost’.

2. Why is the Tundra region is not suitable for people to live comfortably?
Ans: The harsh climatic conditions, scanty vegetation, hibernation of animals, dark months during
winter seasons in Tundra region makes it unsuitable for people to live comfortably.
3. What do you know about the languages of the Eskimos?
Ans: Eskimos languages have been spoken for thousands of years, but they were not written down
until modern days. There are three main languages- Aleut, Yupik and Inupik.
4. Why are the Polar Regions cold throughout the year?
Ans: The Polar Regions are cold throughout the year because they do not get direct sunlight. The sun
is always low at the horizon, even in the middle of summer. In winter, the sun is so far below the
horizon that it doesn’t come up at all for months.
5. Write a short note on seasons in polar region.
Ans: There are two main seasons in the polar region- Summer and Winter.
SUMMER: In the summer, the sun begins to shine in this region around February-March. In the
beginning, the sun shines for a maximum if an hour and a half and then sets. Gradually it increases to
24 hrs. But the Sun doesn’t rise over head; it just hovers a little above the horizon. During this time,
some of the ice melts. The rivers which are frozen during winter melt and begin to flow. When the
summer approaches many multi-coloured plants, lichens, grass, shrubs and berries sprout all around.
WINTER: During winter, it is very cold and dark as the Sun doesn’t rise at all. The months of November,
December and January are very cold and the water becomes ice, strong cold winds blow and there is
heavy snowfall. In this season, the plants die, birds and animals migrate thus making the place
deserted.
